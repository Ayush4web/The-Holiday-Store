Title- Magnificent Amsterdam, Paris & Switzerland

Detailed Day Wise Itinerary :

Day 1 Day 2 Day 3 Day 4 Day 5 Day 6 Day 7 Day 8 Day 9

Day 1- Arrival Amsterdam

Welcome to Europe..!!! On Arrival at Amsterdam Airport, Our Driver will be waiting for your at Arrival hall after Clearing all Formalities Proceed to Hotel. Then after Check into the Hotel Leisure around city.

Day 2- Volendam, Edam & Zaanse Schans Small-Bus Tour

Immerse yourself in the culture of the Netherlands on this amazing tour through the jewels of the Dutch countryside. The tour starts from Amsterdam Central Station and heads to the first destination of the day, Zaanse Schans Spend some time admiring the typical Dutch windmills of the town, and learn about how they are still used today. Next, drive to the Waterland area to arrive at the second stop of the tour, Edam. Walk around some of the town most historical sites, including the town square where the famous cheese market used to take place. Then head to Volendam to visit a local cheese producer and learn from the cheese master about the production of Edam and Gouda cheeses. Head towards a clog factory next to discover the secrets of this typical Dutch shoe. Then, travel back in time to the 17th century as you wander through the fishermen houses in the Marken, and explore this quintessential fishing village.

Day 3- Amsterdam To Paris By Train

Today After Breakfast, Check out from Hotel in Amsterdam and Go to Amsterdam Station on your own. Catch the Train to Paris ( Ticket is Including) Afternoon Reach the and Paris then Check into Hotel.

Day 4- Paris City Sightseen With Big Bus Hop-On Hop-Off Tours (Open-Top) + Ticket for the Top Floor of the Eiffel Tower + Evening Seine River Cruise

You will reach the common point in city where you will leave from, for a complete evening Paris sightseeing tour. Boarding a panomaric bus city tour, you will discover the greatest neighborhoods and Paris landmarks: the Eiffel Tower, the Ile de la Cite and Notre-Dame de Paris, the Invalides, the Latin Quarter and many others. You will see the habits of Parisians and understand how they perceive their city. You will learn the history of Paris and the evolution of its architecture through fascinating anecdotes with individual headphones.Your access to the Eiffel Tower will allow you to climb up to the second floor easily by avoiding as much as possible the long queues at the elevator. You will then reach the second floor of this iconic French monument made by Gustave Eiffel for the 1889 Universal Exhibition. Depending on the time of year, you will see or not the illuminated monuments and will enjoy freely this exceptional 360 panorama. End of the tour at the Eiffel Tower. In the Evening Explore Beautiful Seine River Cruise Seeing the romantic city of Paris by boat is on the must-do list of most visitors to Paris and a Seine cruise is a very popular way to see Paris.

Day 5- Paris To Lucerne by Train

Today in morning after breakfast, check out from Paris hotel and go to train station on own. Catch the train to Paris and reach Lucerne after check in at the hotel and explore Lucerne city.

Day 6- Excursion to Mount Titlis On Swiss Pass with Glacier Cave, Cliff Walk, Ice Flyer and Chair Lift

In morning, just take the train to Engelberg and walk down to the cable car station and there you are. There is no need to participate in a group. On the way back you will have time to visit Lucerne. The old town is very close to the railway station. It is all very compact. Board your coach in Lucerne. Enjoy a breathtaking drive from Engelberg up into the high-alpine glacier world of Titlis Mountain. Enjoy the spectacular sight of the glacier's steep crevasses and gigantic ice boulders from the revolving cable car Rotair. The sun terrace on top of Titlis Mountain (3000 m) offers unforgettable views of the alpine panorama of the Swiss Alps. Visit the Glacier Cave and take a ride with the Ice Flyer-chair lift to the Glacier Park. Pass over Europe's highest suspension bridge and look forward to a summer sleigh ride. Drive back to Lucerne in the late afternoon after an unforgettable winter experience. Overnight stay at Lucerne.

Day 7- Lucerne - Montreux - Geneva - Lucerne (Travel by Train with Swiss Pass)

Today after breakfast, catch the train to Montreux ( 2hr). Montreux is a traditional resort town on lake Geneva. The town promenade is lined with flowers, sculptures, mediterranean trees and grand belle epoque buildings. Offshore is a medieval island castle, Chateau de Chillon, with ramparts, formal halls and a chapel with 14th-century murals. Later Finally in evening catch the train to Geneva. Geneva is a city in Switzerland that lies at the southern tip of expansive Lac Leman (Lake Geneva). Surrounded by the Alps and Jura mountains, the city has views of dramatic Mont Blanc.

Day 8 - Lucerne To Zurich By Train & Rhine Falls + Schaffhausen

Today after breakfast, check out from the hotel in Lucerne and go to Lucerne station on your own. Catch the train to Zurich afternoon and reach Zurich and then check into the hotel. You'll have to change train in Winterthur and get off the train in Neuhausen that is a small town near the falls. After visiting the fall, you can hop again into the train and continue our way to the next station that is Schaffhausen. After the change at Winterthur train station you cross the Rhine river and about 10 minute later the falls. Watching the falls from the window of the train could not convey the real greatness of them. you can got off the train at Neuhausen for enjoy. The whole trip from Zurich to Neuhausen with the time for change is about 1:15 hour. From Neuhausen station to the falls is about 20 minute walk. We suggest you to cross the bridge over the Rhine right in front of the station and follow the signs to the falls through the jungle.That side of the river is more beautiful and when you reach to the fall you will be at a high location which has the best view over the falls. In that side of the falls there is a magnificent observation deck overlooking the fall. For the entrance you have to buy a red ticket that contains the boat ticket for crossing the rive too. But if you want to go into the middle of the falls you have to pay an extra fee for another boat that takes you in to the middle of the falls. After crossing the Rhine by boat, walk back again to the Neuhausen from another side of the river. There is a bus station nearby but we preferred to walk back to Neuhausen and hop on the train to Schaffhausen that was just one station away. The Schaffhausen city is a very beautiful small city near the Rhine.

Day 9 - Departure From Zurich

Today, after a hearty breakfast, after exploring the Swiss like local, It's time to end. Check out from the hotel and transfer to the airport by private car and then take a flight back to home.
